Amazon CloudFront and Amazon S3 are two powerful services from Amazon Web Services (AWS) that work together to provide fast and secure content delivery to users worldwide. This article will discuss the steps to implement Amazon CloudFront with Amazon S3 to enhance content delivery performance and security.

## Step 1: Create a CloudFront Distribution

### 1.1. Log in to the AWS Management Console:

Open a browser and log in to the AWS Management Console with your AWS account.

### 1.2. Open the CloudFront Service:

Click on the "CloudFront" service in the AWS console.

![Step 1.2](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%201.webp?updatedAt=1693891567980)

### 1.3. Create a CloudFront Distribution:

Click the "Create Distribution" button to initiate the distribution creation process.

![Step 1.3](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%202.webp?updatedAt=1693891567974)

## Step 2: Configure the Origin

### 2.1. Add an S3 Bucket as an Origin:

Under the "Origin" section, click "Origin domain" and add an Amazon S3 bucket.

![Step 2.1](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%203.webp?updatedAt=1693891567970)

### 2.2. Configure Cache Settings (Optional):

You can configure cache settings such as TTL (Time To Live) to control how CloudFront will store and manage the cache.

### 2.3. Enable Security Protection (Optional):

You can protect your web application from various types of cyberattacks, including Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks and application-layer attacks like SQL injection, cross-site scripting (XSS), and more.

![Step 2.3](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%204.webp?updatedAt=1693891567774)

## Step 3: Save

### 3.1. Save Configuration:

If you've followed the previous steps, you can save your distribution.

![Step 3.1](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%205.webp?updatedAt=1693891567863)

### 3.2. Wait for Deployment:

The deployment or update process takes 1 to 5 minutes. You can wait until the deployment status is completed.

![Step 3.2](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%206.webp?updatedAt=1693891567719)

### 3.3. Deployment Status:

On the Distributions page, you can see that the status has changed to "enabled," and now you can copy the domain name that has been created.

![Step 3.3](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%207.webp?updatedAt=1693891567893)

## Step 4: Use CloudFront URL

### 4.1. Use S3 URL:

The S3 URL refers to assets stored in Amazon S3. Amazon S3 is a highly scalable object storage service in AWS. The S3 URL directly points to the original source, which can be an image, video, document, or other files stored in an S3 bucket.

![Step 4.1](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%208.webp?updatedAt=1693891567578)

### 4.2. Use CloudFront URL:

Afterward, you'll notice no visible difference, except that the URL provided may appear shorter than before.

![Step 4.2](https://ik.imagekit.io/n1hqrcegw/articles/step-by-step-guide-utilizing-aws-cloudfront-to-accelerate-content-delivery-from-amazon-s3/Frame%209.webp?updatedAt=1693891567843)

## Benefits and Features:

- Amazon CloudFront improves performance by bringing content closer to users worldwide.
- Security features like SSL/TLS encryption are also available to protect your traffic.
- You can optimize the delivery of your web content or applications by configuring cache options and cache behavior.
- CloudFront can help reduce the load on your original S3 server as most requests will be handled by the cache at edge locations.

## Conclusion:

By following the above steps, you can easily implement Amazon CloudFront with Amazon S3 to enhance the performance and security of your content delivery. With global distribution and robust security features, you can provide a better experience to your users worldwide.
